# HackUnion v2 Product Requirements Document

## 1. Product Vision
HackUnion v2 will be the flagship digital identity of HackUnion: a premium, builder-first community platform that helps developers, designers, AI engineers, founders, students, and open-source contributors discover opportunities, collaborate on real projects, and grow together.

The website should create immediate conviction. A visitor should understand, within seconds, that HackUnion is a serious, modern, high-trust community for people who build.

## 2. Mission Statement
HackUnion exists to connect builders with opportunities, people, and momentum through community, collaboration, open source, AI, meetups, hackathons, workshops, and builder experiences.

## 3. Brand Positioning
HackUnion is a modern builder-first technology community for people who learn by building.

It is not a college club, coaching institute, training academy, event agency, or hackathon-only brand. Its position is broader and more durable: a community where people build real things, ship work, and grow together.

## 4. Brand Personality
HackUnion should feel:

- Builder-first
- Modern
- Confident
- Collaborative
- Innovative
- Welcoming
- Human
- Community-driven

It should avoid feeling:

- Corporate
- Academic
- Institutional
- Overly playful
- Generic
- Event-only

## 6. User Personas
### Persona 1: The Student Builder
A student who builds side projects, wants community, and is looking for a place to learn by doing.

Needs:
- A sense of belonging
- Clear ways to join
- Project inspiration
- Events and workshops with real value

### Persona 2: The Independent Developer
A developer who wants collaboration, open-source opportunities, and peers who ship.

Needs:
- High-signal community
- Real opportunities to contribute
- Strong credibility
- Easy access to projects and events

### Persona 3: The AI Builder
An AI engineer or technical creator looking for peers, experiments, and collaborative experiences.

Needs:
- Builder-focused positioning
- Access to technical community experiences
- Proof that HackUnion is current and relevant

### Persona 4: The Founder
A founder or startup-minded builder looking for collaborators, talent, and community energy.

Needs:
- Trust
- High-quality builders
- Partnership opportunities
- Clear value in collaborating with HackUnion

### Persona 5: The Partner / Sponsor
A university, company, or ecosystem partner looking for a credible community to support.

Needs:
- Professionalism
- Impact signals
- Audience clarity
- Partnership model clarity

## 7. User Goals
Users should be able to:
- Understand what HackUnion is and what makes it different
- Join the community or express interest quickly
- Find events, workshops, or builder experiences
- Discover projects, opportunities, and collaborations
- Assess trust, relevance, and legitimacy
- Connect with the right audience path for their intent

## 8. Business Goals
HackUnion v2 should:
- Strengthen brand trust and recognition
- Increase community sign-ups and inbound interest
- Improve conversion for partnerships and collaborations
- Showcase the breadth of HackUnion beyond events
- Support long-term growth through SEO and content discoverability
- Establish a scalable digital foundation for future programs and pages

## 9. Website Objectives
The website must:
- Explain HackUnion clearly and quickly
- Position the brand as premium and builder-first
- Convert different audience groups through tailored paths
- Showcase proof of community momentum and real outcomes
- Support discovery of experiences, projects, and opportunities
- Be accessible, performant, responsive, and SEO-friendly

## 10. Success Metrics
Primary success metrics:
- Community join conversion rate
- Partner inquiry conversion rate
- Event or experience registration conversion rate
- Click-through rate from homepage to key destination pages
- Scroll depth and engagement on key pages

- Returning visitor rate

Supporting metrics:
- Core Web Vitals performance scores
- Accessibility audit pass rate
- Navigation completion rate
- Content interaction rate on featured projects or stories

## 14. Homepage User Journey
A successful homepage journey should follow this sequence:

1. Visitor lands and instantly understands HackUnion as a builder-first community.
2. Visitor sees a strong value proposition and a clear audience fit.
3. Visitor scans proof of activity, credibility, and momentum.
4. Visitor finds a path that matches intent: join, explore projects, view experiences, or partner.
5. Visitor takes a high-confidence action without needing extra explanation.

Homepage should answer:
- What is HackUnion?
- Who is it for?
- Why should I care?
- What can I do next?

## 18. Functional Requirements
The website must:
- Render correctly across desktop, tablet, and mobile
- Support reusable layouts and components
- Present content by audience intent
- Provide clear CTAs on major pages
- Load content quickly and reliably
- Support analytics tracking for major user actions
- Handle SEO metadata per page
- Expose structured content for future scale
- Maintain consistency across pages and templates

## 19. Non-Functional Requirements
The website must meet these non-functional requirements:
- Fast initial load and smooth interactions
- Accessible keyboard and screen reader support
- Responsive behavior on all major breakpoints
- Stable and maintainable architecture
- Clean component boundaries
- Strong SEO foundations
- Secure handling of forms and data
- Easy content extension without redesigning core systems

## 20. Design Principles
- Builder-first before community-first in expression
- Premium, modern, and intentional
- Clear hierarchy over decorative clutter
- High trust through restraint and craftsmanship
- Strong typography and spacing system
- Motion should clarify and elevate, not distract
- The interface should feel original, not derivative
- Every section must earn its presence

## 21. UX Principles
- Make the primary path obvious
- Reduce cognitive load with simple labels and clear structure
- Segment users by intent rather than forcing one path
- Use proof to reduce friction
- Keep interactions predictable and fast
- Support scanning first, reading second
- Design for both first-time visitors and returning community members

## 22. Accessibility Standards
The site must target WCAG 2.2 AA alignment.

Accessibility requirements:
- Full keyboard navigation
- Visible focus states
- Sufficient color contrast
- Semantic HTML structure
- Meaningful alt text for images
- Proper heading hierarchy
- Accessible form labels and errors
- Reduced-motion consideration
- Screen reader-friendly navigation and CTAs

## 23. Performance Standards
Performance targets:
- Fast first contentful paint
- Strong Core Web Vitals performance
- Image optimization through modern formats and responsive sizing
- Minimal client-side overhead
- Motion used sparingly and efficiently
- Avoid unnecessary JavaScript and duplicated dependencies

Engineering expectations:
- Lazy-load non-critical content
- Split code by route and feature where appropriate
- Prefer server-rendered content for SEO-critical pages

## 24. SEO Strategy
SEO should be built in from the start.

Strategy:
- One clear topic per page
- Descriptive titles and meta descriptions
- Clean heading structure
- Indexable content for community, projects, experiences, and partnerships
- Internal linking between related pages
- Structured data where relevant
- Fast loading and mobile-friendly pages
- Search-friendly copy that reflects real user intent

Primary SEO goals:
- Rank for builder community, hackathon community, developer community, open source community, AI builder community, and related intent-based searches
- Support long-tail discovery through stories, projects, and experiences

## 25. Content Strategy
Content should reinforce HackUnion as an active, credible builder ecosystem.

Content pillars:
- Community and belonging
- Projects and open source
- Experiences and events
- Builders and stories
- Partnerships and ecosystem growth

Content rules:
- Write for builders, not institutions
- Use concrete outcomes instead of vague inspiration
- Avoid inflated marketing language
- Show momentum, not just claims
- Make content modular and future-proof

## 26. Motion Design Principles
Motion should feel premium, purposeful, and restrained.

Principles:
- Use motion to guide attention and reinforce hierarchy
- Prefer subtle transitions over flashy effects
- Animate major entrances, page transitions, and emphasis moments
- Keep durations and easing consistent
- Respect reduced-motion preferences
- Motion should support product clarity, not become decoration

## 27. Technical Stack
Recommended stack:
- Next.js with App Router
- React
- TypeScript
- Tailwind CSS
- Framer Motion
- Lucide Icons
- Next Image
- Next Font

Architecture expectations:
- Server-first where possible
- Reusable component-driven design
- Route-level separation for scale
- SEO-friendly rendering
- Performance-conscious implementation

## 28. Folder Structure
The codebase should use a scalable structure such as:
- app/
- components/
- components/ui/
- components/sections/
- components/layout/
- lib/
- data/
- content/
- hooks/
- styles/
- types/
- public/

Guiding principles:
- Group by responsibility
- Keep shared UI primitives isolated
- Keep page-specific sections organized
- Avoid duplicate component patterns

## 29. Component Architecture
Component architecture should support reuse, composition, and long-term maintainability.

Layers:
- UI primitives: buttons, cards, badges, containers, inputs
- Layout components: header, footer, page shell, section wrappers
- Section components: hero, proof, feature grid, CTA band, testimonial/story blocks
- Page compositions: assembled from sections and shared data

Rules:
- No duplicated layouts
- No one-off visual patterns when a reusable pattern exists
- Prefer composition over inheritance
- Make components variant-driven where needed
- Keep components accessible by default

## 30. Data Architecture
Data should be structured for maintainability and scale.

Recommended data domains:
- Navigation items
- Page metadata
- Hero copy variants
- Feature lists
- Project cards
- Experience listings
- Partner logos or profiles
- Story entries
- CTA definitions

Data principles:
- Store structured content separately from presentation where possible
- Use typed data models
- Keep content reusable across pages
- Support future CMS migration without major refactoring

## 31. Coding Standards
Engineering standards:
- TypeScript-first
- Strong typing for props and data models
- Prefer small focused components
- Use descriptive names
- Keep styling patterns consistent
- Avoid duplicated logic
- Use accessible semantics by default
- Keep client-side state minimal unless required
- Maintain predictable file and export conventions

## 32. Security Considerations
Security requirements:
- Sanitize all user-submitted content
- Validate form inputs on the server
- Protect contact and inquiry forms from abuse
- Avoid exposing sensitive data in the client
- Use secure third-party integrations only
- Keep dependencies updated
- Apply secure headers and best practices where appropriate

## 33. Analytics Strategy
Analytics should measure both acquisition and intent.

Track:
- Page views by major route
- CTA clicks
- Join form starts and completions
- Partner inquiry starts and completions
- Scroll depth on homepage and key pages
- Feature card interactions
- Navigation usage
- Search performance and organic landing behavior

Principles:
- Track events aligned to user intent
- Avoid over-collection
- Use analytics to identify drop-off and improve conversion
- Keep consent and privacy considerations in mind

## 34. Launch Checklist
Before launch, confirm:
- Content is complete and approved
- All pages render responsively
- Navigation works across devices
- Forms work and send data correctly
- Metadata is present for all routes
- Open Graph and social metadata are configured
- Images are optimized
- Accessibility checks are passed
- Performance checks are passed
- Analytics events are firing correctly
- Legal pages are present if required
- Broken links and placeholder content are removed
- Motion respects reduced-motion settings

## 35. Future Roadmap
After launch, the roadmap should focus on expansion and depth.

Potential next phases:
- CMS integration
- Member stories and builder profiles
- Event system and registrations
- Project showcase hub
- Resource and learning hub
- Partner portal and sponsorship pages
- Newsletter system
- Community directory
- Multilingual expansion if needed
- Continuous UX and performance optimization

## Closing Principle
HackUnion v2 should never feel like a generic community website. It should feel like the digital home of a serious builder ecosystem: premium, credible, active, and worth joining.
